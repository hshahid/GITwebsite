This March, we're redefining what tech means for high school girls.



What is the MHC GIT Conference?

A mere 15% of the total undergraduate Computer Science degrees are awarded to women. We’ve decided to nip this problem in the bud.

The inaugural Mount Holyoke College Girls in Technology (GIT) Conference aims to target high school girls all over New England at the ripe age of indecisiveness to create awareness among these talented young women about technology and its pervasiveness in almost every field — debunking stereotypes surrounding tech-related industries that may keep girls from venturing into Computer Science. The Conference’s immersion program includes interactive programming and hardware workshops as well as talks by inspirational women who are successfully employing technology in a variety of fields, including, but not limited to, engineering.

Lightning Talks

Inspiring 15-minute talks by women from diverse backgrounds who have made an impact in the tech industry.

Tech Workshops

From programming to hardware, our workshops will be geared towards providing girls with tools to kickstart their engineering careers.

Entrepreneurship

The start-up challenge, with the pitch workshop, is designed to give girls a taste of innovation and the difference they can make through it!
